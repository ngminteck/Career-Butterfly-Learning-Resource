  1. COBOL History:

    • COBOL emerged in the late 1950s and early 1960s as a project sponsored by the United States Department of Defense (DoD). A consortium of computer companies, including IBM, Honeywell, Sperry Rand, and Burroughs, collaborated to create this language.
    • The primary goals were:
      • Portability: COBOL aimed to be portable across different computer systems, making it easier to migrate software between generations of hardware and various manufacturers.
      • English-like Syntax: Unlike other languages of the time (such as FORTRAN), COBOL adopted a more human-readable syntax to encourage a wider audience to program, even if it sacrificed some operational speed.
      • Future-Proofing: COBOL was designed to accommodate future changes.
    • The first official COBOL specifications were released in 1960.
    • Due to its design advantages and strong industry backing, COBOL persisted far beyond its original systems, becoming the most widely used programming language globally by 1970 and running approximately 80 percent of business applications by 1997123.
