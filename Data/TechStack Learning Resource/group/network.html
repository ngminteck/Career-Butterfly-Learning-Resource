In the realm of technology, a network refers to a collection of multiple devices that communicate with each other. It can be as small as two computers or as vast as billions of interconnected devices, including desktops, laptops, tablets, smartphones, and more12.

Here are five free resources where you can enhance your understanding of networks and related topics:

  1. AI Singapore Student Outreach Programme: This initiative promotes AI literacy and proficiency among students. It offers three levels of learning, equipping participants with programming skills and practical experience in AI projects. Completion of each level earns an industry-recognized certificate, and level three participants receive the Google professional machine learning engineer certificate1.

  2. SkillsFuture@NS Learning eXperience Platform: Designed for full-time national servicemen (NSFs), this platform provides free unlimited access to over 75,000 online courses across various categories. NSFs can learn skills like Python programming, machine learning, management training, and digital photography. Some courses may also count for credit exemptions at higher learning institutes1.

  3. Coursera: Coursera offers numerous courses for free, including those from renowned universities. Explore topics like machine learning, game theory, COVID-19 contact tracing, and more. Learn from institutions like Stanford University, Johns Hopkins University, and Imperial College London1.

  4. General Assembly: General Assembly provides free workshops and events on tech-related subjects. While not as extensive as full courses, these sessions offer valuable insights and practical knowledge. Topics cover web development, data science, UX design, and more1.

  5. Mozilla Developer Network (MDN): MDN is a valuable resource for learning web development technologies such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It offers comprehensive documentation, tutorials, and examples for both beginners and experienced developers3.
