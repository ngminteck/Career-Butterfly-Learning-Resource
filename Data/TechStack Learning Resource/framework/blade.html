The Business Logic Attack Definition (BLADE) Framework is an open-source knowledge-base designed to assist cybersecurity professionals in identifying the phases, tactics, and techniques employed by adversaries to exploit weaknesses in the business logic of web-facing systems such as websites and APIs1. Unlike traditional attack frameworks that focus on technology weaknesses, BLADE specifically targets business logic-focused attacks, which are increasingly common. Here are some key points about BLADE:

  1. Purpose: BLADE helps security experts understand and defend against business logic attacks.
  2. Coverage: It captures a comprehensive range of business logic attack types.
  3. Kill Chains: BLADE organizes these attacks into a series of kill chains, allowing proactive defense against automated and business logic-targeted attacks.
  4. Open Source: BLADE is an open-source project, and its goal is to empower defenders to detect and protect against business logic threats2.
