Caffe, short for Convolutional Architecture for Fast Feature Embedding, is a powerful deep learning framework. It was developed by Berkeley AI Research (BAIR), led by Yangqing Jia, during his PhD at UC Berkeley. Here are some key points about Caffe:

  1. Purpose and Features:

    • Caffe is designed with expression, speed, and modularity in mind.
    • It supports various deep learning architectures, including CNNs (Convolutional Neural Networks), RCNNs (Region-based Convolutional Neural Networks), LSTMs (Long Short-Term Memory networks), and fully connected networks 12.
    • Its expressive architecture encourages innovation and application development.
    • Models and optimization are defined through configuration files, avoiding hard-coding.
    • It allows seamless switching between CPU and GPU for training and deployment.
    • The codebase is extensible, fostering active development by a vibrant community.

  2. Speed and Performance:

    • Caffe is known for its speed, making it suitable for both research experiments and industry deployment.
    • It can process over 60 million images per day using a single NVIDIA K40 GPU, achieving impressive inference times (1 ms/image) and learning speeds (4 ms/image) 3.
    • Thanks to contributions from developers, Caffe remains at the forefront of both code and model advancements.

  3. Community and Applications:

    • Caffe powers various applications, from academic research projects to startup prototypes and large-scale industrial systems.
    • The community actively contributes to its development through the caffe-users group and GitHub.
