Certainly! In a nutshell, a fleet refers to a group of ships sailing together, under the same ownership, or engaged in the same activity. It can also denote a collection of vehicles (such as buses, cars, or aircraft) controlled by a single organization or person12.

Here are five free learning resources where you can delve deeper into the concept of fleets:

  1. First Fleet Learning Activities: Explore the journey of the First Fleet, early colony days, Aboriginal perspectives, and profiles of convict women. This unit of work is suitable for Stage 2 students3.

    • First Fleet Learning Activities

  2. EasyTeaching.net: Discover maps, convict registers, reading passages, and comprehension activity sheets related to the First Fleet. Filter results by resource type and grade level4.

    • EasyTeaching.net - First Fleet Resources

  3. Introduction to Fleet Management (E-Learning): A 25-minute online course developed by Fleet Forum and UNHCR. Learn about vehicle life cycle management and considerations for managing humanitarian fleets5.

    • Introduction to Fleet Management

  4. First Fleet Teaching Resources (Teach Starter): Access printable worksheets, slide decks, timelines, and other teaching materials designed for primary school history lessons on the First Fleet6.

    • First Fleet Teaching Resources

  5. NAFA Online Learning and Certification Courses: NAFA, a non-profit organization for fleet managers, offers various online training opportunities. Explore fleet management topics through their courses7.

    • NAFA Online Learning and Certification
